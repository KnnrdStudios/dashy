Cheat Sheet

  • Full Level — forces every pad to velocity 127. Great for recording drums without dynamics.
  • Note Repeat — hold to play repeating notes at the tap-tempo rate; pick 1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 1/32.
  • Arpeggiator — hold chord, it plays the notes in sequence. Use Mode (up/down/random) + Octave + Rate.
  • Tap Tempo — tap 4 times to set the BPM that arpeggiator & note repeat sync to.
  • Joystick — X axis = pitch bend, Y axis = modulation. Centre it when you stop to avoid drift.
  • Prog Change — sends a MIDI program change so your DAW swaps instruments without touching the mouse.
  • CC — assign the 8 knobs to any MIDI CC in the MPK editor to control plugin parameters.

Signal Flow

Audio always moves left→right: the thing you touch last is closest to the speaker.

Gain Staging in 60 seconds

  1. Turn the channel Gain all the way down.
  2. Put on headphones and talk/sing at your loudest realistic level.
  3. Raise the gain until the loudest peaks hit the yellow/green LED, never red.
  4. Set the fader/monitor to a comfortable listening volume — that doesn't change the recording level.
  5. If you add FX (compression, reverb), re-check that peaks still don't clip.

      Pre-Session Checklist

      Tick these off before every recording. Your future self will thank you.

        Level Targets

        • Spoken voice average: -18 to -12 dBFS
        • Peaks never above: -6 dBFS
        • Noise floor below: -60 dBFS
        • On AME2: peaks should briefly touch yellow, never red.

        Mic Technique

        • Distance: one fist (≈ 4 inches) from the mic for a warm, full voice.
        • Angle: speak slightly off-axis (≈ 15°) to reduce plosives.
        • Pop filter: a must if you don't have one built into the mic.
        • Posture: sit up, face forward — consistent distance = consistent tone.
        • Headphones: closed-back only. Open-back will bleed into the mic.

        AME2 Podcast Cheat Sheet

        • Switch to Podcast Mode so auto-tune/voice-FX stay out of your way.
        • Engage Phantom Power (48V) only for condenser mics.
        • Turn on Monitor Mix so you hear yourself with zero latency.
        • Assign sound Pads to intro sting, outro sting, laugh track, applause.
        • Use Ducking if you're playing background music — your voice auto-lowers it.
        • Record a 30-second test and listen back before every real take.
